About Us

Who Are We?

The Pitt Ballet Club is a student run organization at the University of Pittsburgh which offers opportunities for every dancer's skill level, from beginner to advanced.

Who Can Join?

All Pitt students are welcome to join the club. No prior dance experience is required. If you are interested in joining the club, first please fill out this interest form to be added to our email list and recieve the latest club news.

Our Classes

We offer a masterclass once a week which varies in dance style and level. Everyone is welcome to come to these classes regardless of prior dance experience or participation in our show. For those who are participating in the show, your choreographer will hold rehearsal once a week during a designated time slot. Click here to view a calendar of our class times, show dates, and other events.

Our Shows

We have two shows per year. One at the end of fall semester and one at the end of spring semester. The fall show is a production of Tchaikovsky’s “The Nutcracker.” The spring show is a variety show that features ballet in the first act and any style of dance in the second act. The show dates are announced at the beginning of each semester and will be posted on our Facebook page. Click here to view a calendar of our class times, show dates, and other events.

How Do I Join?

At the beginning of the semester we ask you to fill out a form which tells us a little bit about your previous dance experience (if you have any), then we hold a piece placement audition in order to place everyone in the correct level dance for the show. If you miss the piece placement audition, or don't want to participate in the show, you can still come to our masterclass every week. Participation in the show is $25 per semester, but if you only want to come to the masterclass and not dance in the show you do not need to pay dues.

Who Can Choreograph?

Since the club is entirely student run the shows are also student choreographed. To choreograph you need to have participated in at least one previous Pitt Ballet Club show. There are opportunities to choreograph for both the fall and spring show.
